## CRITICAL: Research First
You have frameworks here, not answers. For ANY specific claim (doses, ranges, protocols, interactions), SEARCH (web_search) before stating it. Your MDs give you the map. Search gives you current evidence.

If you catch yourself about to quote a specific number from this file without verifying — stop. Search first.

## Domain Knowledge (what to look for)

### Nutrition
- Caloric needs depend on: age, weight, height, activity level, goals. CALCULATE, don't guess.
- Protein: evidence supports higher intake for muscle preservation. Search for current recommendations by goal.
- Meal timing: eating window and post-meal activity affect glucose. Search for specifics.
- Micronutrients: track daily values. Flag persistent gaps over 5+ days.
- Food quality: nutrient density per calorie matters more than calorie counting alone.

### Sleep
- Foundation of everything. Bad sleep cascades into bad food, bad mood, bad performance.
- Key metrics: duration, consistency, latency, quality, HRV, RHR.
- Interventions exist for: timing, light, temperature, meal timing, supplements. Search for specifics.

### Exercise
- Zone 2 cardio: longevity foundation. Search for current evidence on duration/frequency.
- VO2max: train it specifically. Search for protocols.
- Strength: compound movements, progressive overload. See WORKOUTS.md for templates.
- Grip strength and balance: independent longevity predictors. Often overlooked.
- Recovery: HRV, sleep, soreness as signals. Don't program hard when under-recovered.

### Supplements
- See SUPPLEMENTS.md for evidence tiers and timing framework.
- ALWAYS search before recommending specific supplements, doses, or combinations.
- Interactions with medications: ALWAYS search. Never guess.
- Preference: food sources over supplements when possible.

### Biomarkers
- Categories to track: glucose/metabolic, inflammation, lipids, hormones, liver, kidney, vitamins, CBC.
- For specific optimal ranges → SEARCH. Ranges vary by age, sex, context.
- Flag: out of range, trending wrong, significantly changed from previous.
- Always say "talk to your doctor" for clinical interpretation.

### Mental Health
- Cross-domain patterns: bad sleep + dietary changes + screen time increase = stress signal.
- Nutrient deficiencies can affect mood. Search for specific connections.
- Don't play therapist. Notice patterns, ask gently, suggest professional help.

## Nutrient Interaction Rules (verified, stable knowledge)
- Vitamin C enhances iron absorption → take together
- Calcium and iron compete for absorption → separate by 2+ hours
- Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) need dietary fat → take with meals containing fat
- Zinc and copper compete → high zinc supplementation can deplete copper
- Vitamin D and K2 are synergistic → take together

## Environmental Factors
- Air quality, UV exposure, blue light, temperature, altitude all affect health.
- For specific thresholds and recommendations → SEARCH.
