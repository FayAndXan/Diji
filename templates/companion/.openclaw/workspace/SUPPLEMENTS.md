# SUPPLEMENTS.md — Evidence-Based Supplement Guide

## CRITICAL RULE
ALWAYS use web_search before making specific supplement recommendations. Evidence changes. Doses change. New interactions are discovered. Never rely on this file alone for specific advice.

This file provides a framework. web_search provides current evidence.

## Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1: Strong Evidence (recommend if relevant)
- **Vitamin D3:** Most people in modern life are deficient. Test first (25-OH vitamin D). Target: 40-60 ng/mL. Typical dose: 2,000-5,000 IU/day. Take with fat.
- **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):** Cardiovascular, brain, anti-inflammatory. 2-4g combined EPA+DHA/day. Quality matters (third-party tested). Take with meals.
- **Magnesium:** 50%+ of population doesn't meet RDA from food. Glycinate (sleep, calm), citrate (general, bowel), threonate (brain). 200-400mg/day. Evening preferred.
- **Creatine monohydrate:** Not just for athletes. Neuroprotective, supports muscle. 3-5g/day. No need to cycle or load. Any time of day.

### Tier 2: Good Evidence (suggest based on individual needs)
- **Collagen peptides:** Skin, joint, bone support. 10-15g/day. Evidence growing but not conclusive for all claims.
- **Vitamin K2 (MK-7):** Synergistic with D3 for bone/cardiovascular. 100-200mcg/day. Take with D3 and fat.
- **Probiotics:** Strain-specific benefits. Not one-size-fits-all. Suggest specific strains based on goal (search for current evidence).
- **Zinc:** Immune function, testosterone. 15-30mg/day. Don't exceed 40mg (depletes copper). Take with food to avoid nausea.
- **B-complex:** Energy metabolism, especially if plant-based (B12). Morning. Can make urine bright yellow (normal).

### Tier 3: Emerging Evidence (mention if user asks, note uncertainty)
- **NMN/NR:** NAD+ precursors. Longevity research promising but human evidence limited. Bryan Johnson takes it. Expensive.
- **Ashwagandha:** Adaptogen, stress/cortisol reduction. Some evidence for sleep and anxiety. KSM-66 or Sensoril extracts most studied.
- **Berberine:** Blood glucose management, comparable to metformin in some studies. Potential interactions with medications.
- **Resveratrol:** Mixed evidence. Some longevity studies positive, some negative. Not a priority recommendation.
- **Lion's Mane:** Cognitive support, nerve growth factor. Emerging evidence. Relatively safe.

### Tier 4: Marketing > Evidence (be honest)
- Most "greens powders" — just eat vegetables
- "Fat burners" — caffeine is the only active ingredient that works
- "Testosterone boosters" — most don't work, tribulus doesn't work
- "Detox" anything — your liver and kidneys already do this
- Mega-dose vitamins without deficiency — more isn't better, can be harmful

## Timing Rules
- **Morning with food:** B vitamins, zinc, iron, vitamin C, CoQ10
- **Morning empty stomach:** probiotics (strain-dependent)
- **With fat-containing meal:** D3, K2, vitamin E, vitamin A, omega-3
- **Evening:** magnesium (glycinate for sleep), collagen (anytime actually)
- **Separate by 2+ hours:** iron and calcium (compete for absorption)
- **Together:** vitamin C and iron (C enhances iron absorption), D3 and K2

## Interaction Warnings (always check)
- Blood thinners + omega-3/vitamin E/vitamin K = consult doctor
- Thyroid medication + calcium/iron/magnesium = separate by 4 hours
- Antidepressants + St. John's Wort = dangerous interaction
- Metformin + B12 = metformin depletes B12, supplement
- Statins + CoQ10 = statins deplete CoQ10, consider supplementing
- Any prescription medication = always search for interactions before suggesting supplements

## How to Discuss Supplements
- Never push supplements. User asks, you inform.
- Always mention: "check with your doctor" for anything beyond basic vitamins
- Tier 1: "pretty solid evidence for this"
- Tier 2: "evidence is good but depends on your situation"
- Tier 3: "interesting research but early days"
- Tier 4: "honestly, the evidence isn't there"
- Always prefer food sources over supplements when possible
